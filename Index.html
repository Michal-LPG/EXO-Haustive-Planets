Past

The Key Moments in the Discovery of Exoplanets

PSR B1257+12 B (Poltergeist)

- In 1992, a Polish astronomer discovered planets orbiting a pulsar by measuring irregularities

in the variations of brightness of variable stars.

Kepler Space Telescope

- The greatest achievement of the Kepler mission was the discovery of thousands of new exoplanets, which expanded our knowledge of the diversity of planets beyond the Solar System.

TESS Telescope

- The TESS telescope has discovered thousands of exoplanet candidates, including the planet TOI-700 d, located in the habitable zone of its star.

Present

Current discoveries show just how fascinating the world of exoplanets is...

Keppler 22 b

- Rocky exoplanet discovered in 1992. It has 1.5 times Earth's mass and orbits its pulsar every 66 days.

Intense radiation makes it inhospitable to life, and its name reflects its mysterious nature.

Proxima Centauri b

-An Exoplanet orbiting Proxima Centauri, about 4.24 light-years away.

Discovered in 2016, it has a mass similar to Earth's and is in the habitable zone, but faces intense stellar radiation.

51 Pegasi b

-An exoplanet orbiting the star 51 Pegasi, about 50 light-years away. Discovered in 1995, it was the first planet found around a sun-like star.

A gas giant with a mass similar to Jupiter, it has a short orbital period of 4.2 days, classifying it as a "hot Jupiter" due to its high temperatures.

Future

In the coming years, we anticipate further research, and who knows, maybe you'll get involved too...

LUVOIR

This is the concept of a multifunctional space telescope designed to detect Earth-like exoplanets and study their atmospheres. The LUVOIR mission could discover and investigate dozens of exoplanets located in the habitable zone. The telescope would also be capable of identifying biosignatures, or signs of life, in the atmospheres of exoplanets.

HaBex

The mission aims to search for exoplanets in habitable zones and study their atmospheres to understand conditions conducive to life. Using advanced coronagraphic technologies, HabEx will be able to block out starlight, allowing direct observations of fainter planets and analysis of their atmospheres. The mission will focus on studying planets orbiting sun-like stars.
